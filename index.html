Easy-to-understand information about Horner's Syndrome.

Types of Horner's Syndrome

  • **Central Horner’s Syndrome** - Caused by brainstem or spinal cord injury.
  • **Preganglionic Horner’s Syndrome** - Due to nerve damage between the spinal cord and chest.
  • **Postganglionic Horner’s Syndrome** - Involves damage to nerves near the carotid artery or eye.

Horner's Syndrome Symptoms

  • **Ptosis (Drooping Eyelid)** - Mild drooping due to weak eyelid muscle.
  • **Miosis (Small Pupil)** - Constricted pupil that does not dilate well.
  • **Anhidrosis (Reduced Sweating)** - Less sweating on the affected side.
  • Delayed pupil dilation in dim light.
  • Slight elevation of the lower eyelid.
